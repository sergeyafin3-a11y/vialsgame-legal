Privacy Policy

Vials: ASMR Sand Sort Puzzle
Effective date: 23 June 2026 · Last updated: 24 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Individual Entrepreneur Afinogenov Sergei Olegovich (“we”, “us”, the “Developer”), the publisher of the mobile game Vials: ASMR Sand Sort Puzzle (the “Game”), handles information in connection with your use of the Game.

Short version: the Game is a single-player puzzle. We do not require an account, and we never ask for your name, email address, or precise location. Your game progress is stored on your device and, if you enable cloud backup, in your own Apple or Google account. To run the Game we use a small number of third-party services for purchases, basic usage analytics, optional rewarded advertising, and crash reporting, described in detail below.

1. Who is responsible for your data

The data controller is the Developer named above (an individual entrepreneur registered in the Republic of Armenia, registration / taxpayer No. 2408000572). For any privacy question or request you can contact us at vialsgame.play@gmail.com.

We are established outside the European Economic Area (EEA). The Game does not require an account and collects no special-category data; any processing of EEA users’ data is occasional and limited in scale. On that basis we rely on the exemption in Article 27(2) GDPR and have not appointed an EU/UK representative. If this changes, we will update this policy and appoint one as required.

3. Legal bases for processing (GDPR)

Where the GDPR applies, we process your data on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) — processing in-app purchases and delivering the items you bought.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) — usage analytics and advertising identifiers, where consent is required (e.g. the App Tracking Transparency prompt on iOS and a consent dialog in applicable regions). You may withdraw consent at any time in your device settings.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — crash diagnostics and basic, aggregated analytics needed to keep the Game working and improve it, balanced against your rights.

4. What we do NOT do

  • We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or precise location.
  • We do not require you to create an account with us or log in to play.
  • We do not sell or rent your personal information to anyone.
  • We do not use the analytics or advertising data to build a profile that identifies you personally.

5. Third-party services

The Game relies on these providers for the functions described above:

  • Apple App Store / Google Play — process your payment when you make a purchase. Their handling of your payment data is governed by Apple’s and Google’s own privacy policies.
  • RevenueCat, Inc. — manages and validates in-app purchases on our behalf, using an anonymous app-user identifier and transaction data. See RevenueCat’s Privacy Policy.
  • Google Firebase (Analytics, Crashlytics, Remote Config) — provides aggregated usage analytics, crash diagnostics, and remote configuration. See Firebase’s Privacy & Security information.
  • Google AdMob and its mediation partners (AppLovin, Meta Audience Network, Mintegral) — serve optional rewarded advertising. See Google’s advertising policy.
  • Apple iCloud (iOS) and Google Play Games (Android) — store your cloud-save backup inside your own platform account, governed by Apple’s and Google’s privacy policies.

We are based in the Republic of Armenia, and the third-party providers above (Google, RevenueCat, AppLovin, Meta, Mintegral) are located mainly in the United States and other countries outside your own. Using the Game therefore involves transferring data internationally, including outside the European Economic Area. Where such transfers occur, they are protected by appropriate safeguards — for EEA/UK data, the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses (or the UK equivalent) as implemented by those providers, alongside their own data-transfer frameworks. You can request more detail about these safeguards using the contact below.

7. Children

The Game is suitable for a general audience but is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your country). In parts of the EEA the digital age of consent is higher — up to 16 — and where it applies, the relevant local minimum age governs. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children, and we do not serve personalized (interest-based) advertising. If you believe a child has provided us with information, contact us and we will delete it.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live (e.g. the EEA/UK under GDPR, or California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal information, to object to or restrict certain processing, and to data portability. Because your game data lives on your device, you can exercise the simplest form of this right at any time by deleting the Game, which removes all locally stored data. For purchase records held by the app stores or RevenueCat, contact us at vialsgame.play@gmail.com and we will assist.

EEA/UK: you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (in the UK, the Information Commissioner’s Office) if you believe we have not handled your data lawfully.

California (CCPA/CPRA): we do not “sell” your personal information, and we do not “share” it for cross-context behavioral advertising as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA. We honor opt-out preference signals (such as Global Privacy Control) where required.

9. Data retention

Local game data is kept on your device until you delete it or uninstall the Game. Purchase records are retained by the app stores and RevenueCat for as long as required to provide and support the purchase and to meet legal obligations — in particular, tax and accounting records are kept for the period required by applicable law (typically several years). Analytics and crash data are retained only in aggregated or short-lived form by the relevant provider.

We may update this Privacy Policy as the Game evolves — for example, if we change a third-party provider or add a new feature. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date above and, for material changes, surface a notice in the Game. Continued use after an update means you accept the revised policy.
